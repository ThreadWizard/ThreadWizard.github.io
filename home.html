ThreadWizard — Simple User Guide

ThreadWizard lets you draw a family tree and add DNA match information visually. Think of it like a whiteboard for genealogy, but smarter.

1. The Big Picture

  • Boxes = people
  • Lines = family relationships
  • Extra side lines with labels = DNA comparisons (shared cM, relationship)

Everything happens on one screen.

2. Getting Started

When the page opens, you'll see a basic family tree already on the screen.

Move around

  • Click and drag → move the tree
  • Mouse wheel / trackpad → zoom in and out
  • Fit button → re-center everything

Nothing is permanent — you can experiment freely. Undo mistakes easily.

3. Clicking a Person

Click on any person box to open a panel and add information

This is where almost all editing happens.

You can:

  • Change names
  • Change text size or style
  • Change background color
  • Add relatives
  • Delete the person

4. Building Your Family Tree

Add a child

  1. Click the parent
  2. Type the child's name
  3. Click Add Child

The child appears below the parent.

Add a parent

  1. Click the person
  2. Type the parent's name
  3. Click Add Parent

The tree grows upward.

Add a spouse

  1. Click a person
  2. Type spouse's name
  3. Click Add Spouse

Spouses appear side-by-side.

You can:

  • Swap left/right
  • Remove the spouse later if needed
  • Add another spouse if needed.

5. Re-ordering People

Positioning can give new insights:

  1. Click the person
  2. Use Move Left / Move Right

This changes visual order only — all data stays safe.

6. Minimize Branches

When the tree gets crowded:

  • Collapse Descendants → hides children below a person
  • Collapse Ancestors → hides parents above a person

Hover over a box to see the collapse icon.

You can always expand again later. "Expand all" in the tools panel makes this super easy.

7. Adding DNA Comparisons

This is what makes ThreadWizard different.

How to add a DNA match

  1. Click a dot to select a match/comparison
  2. A DNA Comparison panel opens

Now enter:

  • Shared cM (example: 225 cM)
  • Relationship (example: aunt, second cousin, 3C1R)

Click Save

A label appears on the line.

Helpful extras

  • The tool reports the relationship automatically from the tree, but you can modify it.
  • The two people glow when you hover over their McGuire line (DNA line).
  • You can style the cM labels (size, color, bold)

8. Understanding the Colored DNA Lines

If enabled:

  • Blue = small match
  • Green → Yellow → Orange → Red = increasing shared DNA

This helps spot stronger vs weaker matches at a glance.

You can turn this on/off in Tools.

9. Tools Button (Global Controls)

Click Tools at the top to adjust everything at once:

You can:

  • Change spacing between generations
  • Change box size
  • Change McGiuire line (DNA line) spacing
  • Change text styling for all people
  • Export images

There is also a shading tool to highlight regions of the chart visually.

10. Saving, Undo, and Safety

You are protected from mistakes:

  • Undo / Redo buttons work
  • The tree auto-saves in your browser

11. Exporting Your Work

You can:

  • Export JSON → reopen later or share with another user
  • Download PNG → image file for sharing, presentations, or research notes
  • Download SVG → for high-quality editing or publication

12. What This Tool Is Best For

Visualizing complex cousin relationships DNA triangulation thinking Seeing multiple matches across branches Teaching or explaining family connections

A complement to your other DNA tools.

One-Sentence Summary

ThreadWizard lets you draw a family tree and DNA relationships in a way that makes complex connections easy to see.
